Places

[Non-fiction]

A directory of institutions, some centuries old and others brand new, all with archives of relevant reports that are accessible to the general public. For people who like to track sources and read footnotes.

All images were taken from National Institute of Standards and Technology Digital Collections, Gaithersburg, MD 2089

CRS: Congressional Research Service

Founded: 1914 [orig. Legislative Reference Service, US gov.]

Resources: Summaries of US policy on current issues--- Can we legally give robots guns?

Org Bio: "By law, CRS works exclusively for Congress, providing timely, objective, and authoritative research and analysis to committees and Members of both the House and Senate, regardless of political party affiliation. As a legislative branch agency within the Library of Congress, CRS has been a valued and respected resource on Capitol Hill for more than a century."

Reports Directory: https://www.congress.gov/help/crs-products

Example reports: AI Vocabulary Guide, AI in Healthcare, AI Weapons Systems, AI in Elections

Image Credit: "U.S. Capitol dome, exterior"

AI Incident Database:

Founded: 2021 [nonprofit, NGO]

Resources: Searchable directory of articles about AI misuse and failure -- Crash reports for AI

Org Bio: "The AI Incident Database is dedicated to indexing the collective history of harms or near harms realized in the real world by the deployment of artificial intelligence systems. Like similar databases in aviation and computer security, the AI Incident Database aims to learn from experience so we can prevent or mitigate bad outcomes."

Reports Directory: https://incidentdatabase.ai

Example reports: Deepfake Trump Ads for Rebate Scam, Nomi AI Directs User to Stab Father

Image Credit: "Seat belt testing"

ML Commons:

Founded: 2018. [nonprofit, NGO]

Resources: Benchmarking of AI performance and safety --- Leaderboard in the AI Space Race

Org Bio: "Democratizing new technological capabilities and ensuring wide-spread adoption requires an open approach. MLCommons’ collective engineering continually measures and improves the accuracy, safety, speed, and efficiency of AI technologies."

Reports Directory: https://mlcommons.org/research/

Example reports: Scoring AI Safety and Reliability, Scoring AI Energy Efficiency

Image Credit: "Measures for Progress: The New World of Science"

NIST: National Institute of Standards and Technology

Founded: 1901 [orig. National Bureau of Standards, US gov.]

Resources: Best practices for AI, cybersecurity, biosecurity, privacy -- Setting the standards

Org Bio: "Heeding the call from the nation's scientists and industrialists to establish an authoritative domestic measurement and standards laboratory, the US Congress founded NIST. The fledgling agency quickly assembled standards for electricity, length and amss, temperature, light and time, and created a system to transfer those values to the public."

Reports Directory (AI): https://www.nist.gov/news-events/news-updates/tag/2810121

Example reports: AI Agent Hijacking, The NIST AI Risk Management Framework

Image Credit: "(New) Radium Solution Standards"

Concept Art Association:

Founded: 2023 [NGO]

Resources: Entertainment industry advocates -- Human art for human audiences

Org Bio: We are the Concept Art Association, an advocacy organization for artists working in entertainment. Our community have been leaders in our industry fighting back against the unethical practices happening in the Generative AI space. As an organization and as individuals we deeply care about this issue, not just for those actively working as visual artists, but for future generations of artists and for the preservation of our creative industries.

Reports Directory: https://www.conceptartassociation.com/advocacy

Example reports: Impact of AI on Entertainment Industry (2024)

Image Credit: "A half century of delivering accurate time and frequency by radio"

TDAI: Translational Data Analytics Institute

Founded: 2014 [university research center]

Resources: Diverse talks, research, grants--- Translating data into action to benefit the world

Org Bio: "An intellectual community at the forefront of interdisciplinary, AI- and data science-enabled research and creative expression for the greater good. TDAI spans more than 800 faculty and 50 disciplines at Ohio State University."

Grants Directory: https://tdai.osu.edu/research-action/tdai-research-pilot-teams

Example Grants: Predicting Preeclampsia from EHR data, Towards Fair Automated Hiring in Practice, Interpretable AI Medical Diagnosis, AI Remote Sensing to Halt Deforestation in Africa.

Image Credit: "The self and mutual-inductances of linear conductors"

IFP: Institute for Progress

Founded: 2022 [think tank]

Resources: Proposals for how society could fully leverage the power of AI --- Pitching the future

Org Bio: The future we got is much weirder and more uneven than the one that we were promised. The time is ripe for new (and renewed) institutions in the United States. We need to build new science-funding agencies, pandemic prevention systems, and even new think tanks.

Reports Directory: https://ifp.org/latest-publications/

Example reports: Making AI Scientists, Controlling Data Markets for AI Training

Image Credit: "High Speed Quantum Key Distribution Over Optical Fiber Network System"

Epoch AI:

Founded: 2021 [nonprofit, NGO]

Resources: Short, easy reports on AI capabilities, forecasts for what's next -- AI Weather Report

Org Bio: Epoch AI is a multidisciplinary non-profit research institute investigating the future of artificial intelligence. We examine the driving forces behind AI and forecast its economic and societal impact. Our goal is to create a healthy scientific environment, where claims about AI are discussed with the rigor they merit.

Reports Directory: https://epoch.ai/blog

Example reports: Evaluating Gemini's Math Abilities, What will AI look like in 2030?, How will AI automation impact the economy?

Image Credit: "Wind pressure on a model of the empire state building"

FPF: Future of Privacy Forum

Founded: 2008 [nonprofit, NGO]

Resources: Accessible summaries of global legal and technical topics --- What's happening where?

Org Bio: FPF brings together industry, academics, consumer advocates, and other thought leaders from across the world to explore the challenges posed by technological innovation and develop privacy protections, ethical norms, and workable business practices.

Reports Directory: https://fpf.org/resources/

Example reports: Chatbot personalities, AI Unlearning, AI Regulation in Latin America, US State-level AI Regulation

Image Credit: "Path antenna gain in exponential atmosphere"

Founded: 1994. [nonpofit, NGO]

Resources: Advocacy in internet, privacy, technology, civil rights --- How do we play nice?

Org Bio: CDT is a champion of global online civil liberties and human rights, driving policy outcomes that keep the internet open, innovative, and free.

Reports Directory: https://cdt.org/insights/

Example reports: Implementing EU AI Regulation, Impact of AI in US Schools, Open Letter to DOE

UK-AISI: Artificial Intelligence Security Institute

Founded: 2023 [U.K. gov.]

Resources: Reports on safety and security vulnerabilities in AI --- Where could things go bad?

Org Bio: Improving our understanding of advanced AI: Our mission is to equip governments with a scientific understanding of the risks posed by advanced AI. The AI Security Institute is a research organisation within the UK Government’s Department for Science, Innovation, and Technology.

Reports Directory: https://www.aisi.gov.uk/work

Example reports: Data Poisoning, Vulnerable Open Models, What do Chatbots tell voters?

Image Credit: "Parallel reversible permeability measurement techniques"

Open AI:

Founded: 2015 [private company]

Resources: Research papers documenting major updates to OpenAI Tech --- The newest AI

Org Bio: Our mission is to ensure that artificial general intelligence—AI systems that are generally smarter than humans—benefits all of humanity. We are building safe and beneficial AGI, but will also consider our mission fulfilled if our work aids others to achieve this outcome.

Reports Directory: https://arxiv.org/search/?query=openai

Example reports: Competitive Programming with Large Reasoning Models, Newest Open Models

Image Credit: "Instrument Control (iC) An Open-Source Software to Automate Test Equipment"

HAI: Human Centered AI

Founded: 2019 [university research center]

Resources: Five years of index reports tracking wtf has been happening --- Making sense of it all

Org Bio: The Stanford Institute for Human-Centered AI (HAI) is an interdisciplinary institute established in 2019 to advance AI research, education, policy, and practice. Stanford HAI brings together thought leaders from academia, industry, government, and civil society to shape the development and responsible deployment of AI.

Reports Directory: https://hai.stanford.edu/ai-index/2025-ai-index-report [scroll all the way down]

Example reports: 2025 AI Index report, 2023 AI Index Report, 2021 AI Index Report
