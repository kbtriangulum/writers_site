Workshops

[Non-fiction -> Fiction]

The Doors Workshops are meant to open a more direct pathway between research and imagination. Below are accessible summaries of real presentations from cutting edge technology and policy conferences, with responses written by the fiction, entertainment and arts communities. Take a look through to see where we might be going next.

All images are taken from National Institute of Standards and Technology Digital Collections, Gaithersburg, MD 2089

Curious Robot Children

Logline: Toddlers use all their senses to experience things that interest them, creating self-directed, well-organized data. Allowing AI to have bodies, curiosity and intention may make training new AI much easier and quicker.

Original talk: "Coherence statistics, self-generated experience and why young humans are much smarter than current AI". Linda Smith, Indiana University [Neurips 2023].  [Lab Website]

How to Pay Attention

Democratizing Access to AI

Logline: Making a smart AI requires data and computation power at an incredible, unsustainable scale. With improvements to architecture and training, though, it might be possible to train AI on everyday consumer technology. Creating new AI might be open to all.

Original talk: "NextGenAI: The Delusion of Scaling and the Future of Generative AI", Björn Ommer, University of Munich
